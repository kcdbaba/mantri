## RESEARCH CONTEXT

I am building an AI agent for a small Army supply business in Guwahati, India. The agent reads WhatsApp conversations (in Hinglish — Hindi written in Roman script mixed with English) from multiple chat groups and extracts a unified task list with suggested next steps. The business owner (Ashish) manages operations remotely; staff coordinate with suppliers and clients on the ground.

My primary quality risk is: **the agent misses procedural/cadence implicit tasks** — subtasks that should happen at a certain stage of every order regardless of what anyone writes in WhatsApp (e.g. pre-dispatch checklist, post-delivery payment confirmation, monthly reconciliation). The agent correctly infers reactive implicit tasks (supplier silence → follow-up) but fails on procedural ones because they leave no message-based signal.

My secondary concern is **trust and adoption** — whether staff will act on the agent's alerts and whether Ashish will rely on it for operational decisions.

## ANALYSIS INSTRUCTIONS

### For ALL transcripts:

**1. Current workflow signals**
Extract concrete descriptions of how they currently track tasks, follow up with suppliers/clients, and handle coordination. Note any workarounds, manual tracking methods, or informal systems mentioned.

**2. Pain point evidence**
Extract specific frustrations, time sinks, error-prone areas, and failure modes. Include direct quotes. Note emotional language (frustration, stress, overwhelm).

**3. Quality risk validation**
For each of the 6 risks below, identify whether the transcript contains evidence that validates, contradicts, or is neutral on the risk. Include the direct quote that supports your assessment.

| Risk | Look for |
|---|---|
| **Cadence implicit tasks** | Mentions of steps that "always happen" but are never in WhatsApp; procedures done from memory or habit |
| **Staff quality variance** | Tasks certain staff "always need reminding about"; roles where things fall through most often |
| **Missing WhatsApp entries** | "We sorted it on the phone", "I told her in person", verbal instructions that bypass chat |
| **Warehouse inventory blindness** | "That one we have in stock", orders filled without supplier contact |
| **Trust threshold** | How much verification they want before acting on an agent suggestion; tolerance for false alarms |
| **Off-platform instruction gap** | Ashish or staff giving instructions verbally that never appear in any group chat |

**4. Next best alternative**
What do they currently use or do instead of an agent? (WhatsApp pins, personal notes, memory, Excel, nothing.) What bar does the agent need to clear?

**5. Trust and adoption signals**
What would make them trust the agent? What would make them ignore or switch it off? What level of control do they need?

**6. Surprises**
Any finding that was not anticipated — something they said that challenges an existing assumption or reveals a need not previously identified.

---

### Additional instructions for STAFF transcripts only:

**7. Privacy and comfort signals**
Did they express any discomfort about which chats the agent reads? Any chats or topics they'd want excluded?

**8. Override and control needs**
Did they describe situations where they'd want to correct the agent or tell it to back off? How would they want to do that?

**9. Noise tolerance**
How many alerts per day would feel helpful vs overwhelming? Did they give any signal about the right volume or cadence of reminders?

---

### Additional instructions for ASHISH transcripts only:

**7. Task taxonomy inputs**
For each order type discussed, extract:
- The sequence of steps Ashish described (in order)
- Steps he identified as frequently skipped or delayed
- Steps that happen off-platform (phone/in-person only)
- Who handles each step (Ashish vs which staff member)

**8. Design decisions**
Extract any statements that inform architecture or design choices:
- Automation threshold (when does he want to approve vs let the agent act?)
- Staff risk sensitivity (is he comfortable with differentiated alert thresholds per staff?)
- Inventory integration (is he willing to provide a stock list?)
- Correction UX (how does he want to tell the agent it's wrong?)

**9. Edge cases captured**
List any edge cases or exceptions Ashish raised that don't fit the standard workflow. These feed directly into the task taxonomy documentation.

**10. Customer priority signals**
What did Ashish say he most wants the agent to do well? What would make him confident enough to rely on it during a busy week? What would make him stop using it?

**11. Router design parameters**
For each parameter below, extract Ashish's stated preference or threshold exactly as he described it. If not discussed, mark as [NOT DISCUSSED].

| Parameter | Q# | What to extract |
|---|---|---|
| Wrong-routing tolerance | Q2 | Does he want the agent to flag routing uncertainty visibly, or try silently and let him correct later? |
| Context boundary gap threshold | Q6 | How fast does conversation shift between orders in the All-Staff group — several per day, or one/two topics at a time? |
| Supplier silence threshold | — | At what point (hours/days) does he want the agent to flag a supplier as unresponsive? |
| Clarification vs carry-forward | Q11 | When the agent is unsure which order a message belongs to: ask to confirm, or make best guess and flag uncertainty? |
| Concurrent same-item order frequency | Q12 | How often are two active orders for the same item type running simultaneously? |
| Group alias bootstrapping sufficiency | Q13 | Will 6 months of chat history be enough for the agent to pick up team shorthand, or does it need manual seeding? |
| Cross-group reference convention | Q14 | Does he refer to orders by client-group name or supplier-entity name when discussing in All-Staff/supplier groups? |
| Staff clarification willingness | Q15 | Will staff resolve routing ambiguity themselves, or escalate to Ashish? Preferred channel? |
| Silent expiry policy | Q16 | If a message can't be resolved after a few follow-on messages, is quietly dropping it acceptable? Or does he want a clarification prompt? |
| Officer alias persistence | Q10 | How long do officer contacts typically stay at a posting? How is the transition handled? |
