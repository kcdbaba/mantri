## TASK TYPE 1: Standard Equipment Procurement
# Examples: tandoor, AC, fridge, furniture, general equipment
# Base case — all other types are variants of this.

Expected subtask sequence:
1.  Enquiry received / initial client request logged           [CONFIRMED]
2.  Quote requested from supplier                             [CONFIRMED]
    - If supplier unresponsive 48h: follow-up task           [CONFIRMED]
3.  Quote received (rate + GST + delivery charges)            [CONFIRMED]
4.  Quote prepared and sent to client                         [CONFIRMED]
    - If client unresponsive 24h: follow-up task             [CONFIRMED]
5.  Order confirmed by client                                 [CONFIRMED]
6.  PO issued to supplier                                     [CONFIRMED]
7.  Advance payment to supplier (if required)                 [UNVALIDATED - depends on supplier]
8.  Delivery date committed by supplier                       [CONFIRMED]
    - Set conservative internal tracking milestone            [CADENCE - UNVALIDATED buffer rules]
      Local supplier: internal milestone = committed date
      Inter-state supplier (Malerkotla, Delhi): internal milestone = committed date + 1-2 days
      Adjust further based on supplier's past reliability
    - Pre-emptive reminder to supplier (1-2 days before deadline) [CADENCE - UNVALIDATED timing]
      Inter-state / long lead time: reminder 2 days before
      Local supplier: reminder 1 day before
    - If delivery date passes without dispatch: escalation   [CONFIRMED]
9.  Goods dispatched by supplier                              [CONFIRMED]
10. Goods received at Ashish's end (warehouse / transit hub) [CONFIRMED]
11. Inspection and sorting                                    [CADENCE - rarely logged in WhatsApp]
12. Pre-dispatch checklist completed                          [CADENCE - almost never in WhatsApp]
13. Delivery to client arranged (transport / vehicle)         [CONFIRMED]
14. Delivery to client completed                              [CONFIRMED]
15. Client acceptance / sign-off                              [CADENCE - often only verbal]
16. Invoice raised                                            [CONFIRMED]
17. Payment follow-up (if not received within 30 days)        [CADENCE - frequently missed]
18. Payment received                                          [CONFIRMED]
19. GST reconciliation                                        [CADENCE - monthly, rarely in WhatsApp]

Frequently skipped or delayed (from SATA case evidence):
- Step 11 (inspection): goods often sorted informally, not logged
- Step 12 (pre-dispatch checklist): no evidence this appears in any chat
- Step 15 (client sign-off): verbal acknowledgement, not always written
- Step 17 (payment follow-up): if payment is delayed, follow-up is often absent

## TASK TYPE 2: Custom / Made-to-Order Items
# Examples: flags, printed signage, embroidered items, custom uniforms, nameplates
# Key difference from standard: spec confirmation and sample approval are critical gates

Expected subtask sequence:
1.  Enquiry received with initial specification                [CONFIRMED]
2.  Spec confirmation with client                             [CADENCE - must happen before quoting]
    - Size, material, design, quantity, Army unit markings
    - Written spec document or WhatsApp confirmation required
3.  Sample / design proof approval (if applicable)            [UNVALIDATED - not seen in test cases]
4.  Quote from manufacturer (includes production time)        [CONFIRMED]
5.  Quote sent to client                                      [CONFIRMED]
6.  Order confirmed + final spec locked                       [CADENCE - spec must be locked at this point]
7.  Production timeline committed by manufacturer             [CONFIRMED]
    - If timeline at risk: follow-up task                    [UNVALIDATED]
8.  Production follow-up at midpoint                          [CADENCE - proactive check, rarely in WhatsApp]
9.  QC inspection of finished goods before dispatch           [CADENCE - critical for custom items]
10. Delivery to client                                        [CONFIRMED]
11. Client acceptance / rejection handling                    [CADENCE - rejection risk higher for custom]
    - If rejected: rework or replacement flow                [UNVALIDATED]
12. Invoice raised                                            [CONFIRMED]
13. Payment follow-up                                         [CADENCE]
14. Payment received                                          [CONFIRMED]

Frequently skipped or delayed:
- Step 2 (spec confirmation): clients often give verbal specs; no written record
- Step 9 (QC before dispatch): goods shipped without formal QC check
- Step 11 (client acceptance): rejection often surfaces only after delivery

## TASK TYPE 3: Army Direct Delivery
# Examples: delivery direct to Army unit / cantonment, client arranges transport from warehouse
# Key difference: Army-specific logistics, gate passes, unit coordination

Expected subtask sequence:
1-8.  Same as Standard Equipment Procurement (steps 1–10)     [CONFIRMED]
9.    Coordinate delivery logistics with Army unit             [CADENCE]
      - Confirm delivery address + unit / department
      - Identify receiving officer or contact
10.   Gate pass / entry documentation arranged                 [CADENCE - Army-specific; often overlooked]
11.   Pre-dispatch checklist completed                         [CADENCE]
12.   Delivery vehicle arranged or Army transport confirmed    [CONFIRMED]
13.   Dispatch confirmation                                    [CONFIRMED]
14.   Delivery confirmation from Army unit                     [CONFIRMED]
      - Written acknowledgement (DO / GRN) often required
15.   DO / GRN / signed delivery note received                 [CADENCE - required for GeM/formal payment]
16.   Invoice raised (with DO reference if required)           [CONFIRMED]
17.   Payment follow-up                                        [CADENCE]
18.   Payment received                                         [CONFIRMED]

Frequently skipped or delayed:
- Step 10 (gate pass): arranged verbally, never logged in WhatsApp
- Step 15 (DO / GRN receipt): follow-up often required; delays invoice cycle

## TASK TYPE 4: GeM Portal Orders
# Examples: orders placed by Army units through Government e-Marketplace
# Key difference: portal-specific workflow steps; payment via GeM, not direct

Expected subtask sequence:
1.  GeM order received (portal notification)                  [CONFIRMED]
2.  Order details verified on portal (item, qty, price)       [CADENCE - must be done before accepting]
3.  GeM order accepted on portal (within response window)     [CADENCE - portal deadline; missing = auto-rejection]
4.  Supplier sourcing (same as standard procurement)          [CONFIRMED]
5.  Delivery timeline committed on portal                     [CADENCE - portal requires this update]
6.  Goods procured and dispatched (standard flow)             [CONFIRMED]
7.  Delivery confirmation uploaded to portal                  [CADENCE - frequently missed; blocks payment]
8.  Invoice raised on portal (e-invoice required)             [CADENCE - portal-specific format]
9.  GeM payment timeline tracked (30/60/90 day cycle)         [CADENCE - different from direct client payment]
10. Payment received via GeM portal                           [CONFIRMED]
11. GST reconciliation (GeM has its own GST flow)             [CADENCE - distinct from standard]

Frequently skipped or delayed:
- Step 3 (portal acceptance window): if missed, order auto-cancels
- Step 7 (delivery confirmation on portal): goods delivered but portal not updated; blocks payment
- Step 8 (e-invoice on portal): physical invoice insufficient for GeM payment
- Step 9 (payment tracking): GeM payment cycle longer than direct; follow-up process different

## TASK TYPE 5: Inter-State Procurement
# Examples: Malerkotla (Punjab) suppliers, Delhi suppliers, other out-of-state
# Key difference: transport leg, transit time, additional documentation

Expected subtask sequence:
1-6.  Same as standard procurement (enquiry → PO)             [CONFIRMED]
7.    Transporter identified and booked                        [CADENCE]
8.    Transporter briefed on pickup address + timeline         [CADENCE]
9.    E-way bill / transport documentation arranged            [CADENCE - required for interstate goods]
10.   Goods dispatched by supplier to transporter              [CONFIRMED]
11.   Transit follow-up (particularly for long haul)           [CADENCE - Malerkotla to Guwahati is 3-4 days]
12.   Expected arrival date tracked                            [CADENCE]
      - If delayed: escalation task                           [CONFIRMED from Malerkotla case]
13.   Goods received at Guwahati                               [CONFIRMED]
14.   Transit damage inspection                                [CADENCE - higher risk for interstate]
15.   Steps 11–19 of standard procurement                      [CONFIRMED]

Frequently skipped or delayed:
- Step 9 (e-way bill): arranged informally; documentation gaps are compliance risk
- Step 11 (transit follow-up): transporter goes silent; no updates until arrival or problem
- Step 14 (transit damage inspection): informal; goods unpacked before formal check

## TASK TYPE 6: Stock-Filled Orders
# Examples: commonly stocked items fulfilled from Ashish's warehouse without supplier contact
# Key difference: no supplier sourcing step; stock must be checked and deducted

Expected subtask sequence:
1.  Enquiry / order received                                  [CONFIRMED]
2.  Stock check — item and quantity available?                [CADENCE - must happen before committing]
3.  Stock availability confirmed to client                    [CADENCE]
4.  Delivery arranged from warehouse                          [CONFIRMED]
5.  Pre-dispatch checklist completed                          [CADENCE]
6.  Goods dispatched                                          [CONFIRMED]
7.  Stock deduction logged                                    [CADENCE - frequently missing; causes inventory blindness]
8.  Delivery to client confirmed                              [CONFIRMED]
9.  Invoice raised                                            [CONFIRMED]
10. Payment follow-up                                         [CADENCE]
11. Payment received                                          [CONFIRMED]
12. Inventory replenishment check (if stock low post-order)   [CADENCE - proactive; almost never in WhatsApp]

Frequently skipped or delayed:
- Step 2 (stock check): assumed rather than confirmed; risk of committing unavailable stock
- Step 7 (stock deduction): most likely to be missing; critical for inventory accuracy
- Step 12 (replenishment check): reactive rather than proactive; stock-outs surface only at next order

## TASK TYPE 7: Service Orders (Repairs / Maintenance)
# Examples: equipment repair, maintenance contracts, installation services
# Key difference: no goods movement; scope confirmation and job sign-off are the key gates

Expected subtask sequence:
1.  Service request received (description of issue)           [CONFIRMED]
2.  Site visit / assessment scheduled                         [CADENCE - timing often not logged]
3.  Site visit completed; diagnosis confirmed                 [CADENCE - outcome often only verbal]
4.  Scope of work and parts list confirmed with client         [CADENCE]
5.  Quote for parts + labour                                  [CONFIRMED]
6.  Client approval of quote                                  [CONFIRMED]
7.  Parts sourcing (if required) — standard procurement flow  [CONFIRMED]
8.  Service date scheduled                                    [CADENCE]
9.  Service / repair executed                                 [CONFIRMED]
10. Job completion sign-off from client                       [CADENCE - verbal only; no written record common]
11. Invoice raised (parts + labour)                           [CONFIRMED]
12. Payment follow-up                                         [CADENCE]
13. Payment received                                          [CONFIRMED]
14. Warranty / follow-up period tracked (if applicable)       [CADENCE - UNVALIDATED]

Frequently skipped or delayed:
- Step 3 (diagnosis confirmed): engineers give verbal diagnosis; client later disputes scope
- Step 10 (job sign-off): completion assumed without written confirmation
- Step 14 (warranty tracking): no mechanism to track post-service issues

## TASK TYPE 8: Collections and Payment Follow-Up
# Not a separate order type — a recurring cadence task that runs in parallel with all other types.
# Listed separately because it is the most commonly missed procedural step.

Expected cadence:
- Day 0:    Invoice raised; due date communicated to client     [CONFIRMED]
- Day 30:   First follow-up if payment not received             [CADENCE - most commonly missed]
- Day 45:   Second follow-up (more direct)                     [CADENCE]
- Day 60:   Escalation to Ashish for direct intervention        [CADENCE]
- Day 90:   Formal notice or account hold (if applicable)       [UNVALIDATED]
- On receipt: Payment received confirmation sent to client      [CONFIRMED]
- On receipt: Receipt / acknowledgement issued                  [CADENCE - often skipped]
- Monthly:  GST reconciliation across all receipts              [CADENCE - monthly; almost never in WhatsApp]
- On invoice (large value, long payment terms): Invoice financing check [CADENCE - TBC with Ashish: value + term thresholds]

Frequently skipped:
- Day 30 follow-up: the single most common missed procedural step
- Receipt acknowledgement: payment logged internally but client not notified
- GST reconciliation: happens offline; no chat signal at all

## TYPE 9: Proactive Order Sourcing
*(Ashish proactively reaches out to existing clients to generate new orders — not triggered by any incoming enquiry)*

This is NOT an order-processing type. It is a business development cadence — Ashish's business does not passively wait for enquiries. Regular client outreach is part of normal operations.

| Trigger | Expected action | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Client silent for N weeks | **Check in with client** — "kuch chahiye tha?" | [UNVALIDATED — N weeks TBC with Ashish] |
| Seasonal / procurement cycle known | **Proactive quote push** — lead with relevant items for the season | [UNVALIDATED — which clients, which seasons] |
| Recent delivery completed | **Follow-up call** — confirm delivery satisfaction, probe for next requirement | [UNVALIDATED] |
| New item available from supplier | **Outreach to relevant clients** — inform of new stock/item | [UNVALIDATED] |

Typical signal in WhatsApp: Ashish messages a client group with no prior incoming message — "sir sab theek hai? kuch chahiye ho toh batao". Or no WhatsApp signal at all (phone call only).

Implicit task detection rule: if a client has had no interaction in the client group for > N weeks and all prior orders are completed, surface a proactive outreach task.
